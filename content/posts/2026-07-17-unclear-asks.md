title: Stakeholders don't resist change. They resist unclear asks.
type: note
tags: [BA-practice, stakeholders]

A thing I keep re-learning on every project: the requirement wasn't rejected — it was never actually stated.

When a stakeholder pushes back, my first check is no longer "how do I convince them?" It's "can they repeat the ask back to me in their own words?" Nine times out of ten, they can't — and that's the real blocker.

Clarity first. Persuasion second.
